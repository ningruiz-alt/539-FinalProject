Game Library

Genres

These are the games that I played and enjoyed! I briefly seperated them into three genres based on my experience, you can give me advices if you have any thoughts!

Open world · Soulslike

Elden Ring

Taught me to enjoy getting lost and failing in strange, beautiful places instead of rushing to the ending.

Open world · Story

Red Dead Redemption 2

Made my first trip to the Rockies feel strangely familiar, like I had already ridden those trails before.

RPG · Party · Choice

Baldur's Gate 3

A tabletop-style campaign where every decision becomes group drama.

RPG · Story · Choice

The Witcher 3

Side quests that often feel more meaningful than the main story line.

Narrative · Time rewind

Life is Strange

A concentrated package of teenage drama I never had in real life.

War · Survival

This War of Mine

Forces me to see war from civilians' daily struggles instead of epic battles.

Episodic · Zombies

The Walking Dead

A series where I constantly question whether I picked the “right” kind of cruel.

Action · Mythology

Black Myth: Wukong

Made me look at real Chinese temples and Journey to the West with new eyes, noticing structure and symbolism more carefully.

Open world · Sci-fi

Cyberpunk 2077

Let me walk a city where every alley feels like a different story about technology, class and power.

Open world · Exploration

Death Stranding

Turned walking and delivering packages into a slow, meditative way of thinking about distance and connection.

Co-op · Puzzle

It Takes Two

A game that forces constant communication—every mechanic is designed to make two people pay attention to each other.

Tactical · Team

Rainbow Six Siege

Stayed with me through undergrad and reconnected me with high school friends through late-night ranked games.

Co-op · Boss hunts

Monster Hunter

Taught me how satisfying it is to learn monster patterns together and celebrate tiny improvements after each hunt.
