Genres

These are the games that I played and enjoyed! I briefly seperated them into three genres based on my experience, you can give me advices if you have any thoughts!

Open world · Soulslike

Elden Ring

The easist soulslike game but still pretty hard. What is your choice in a collapasing world?

Open world · Story

Red Dead Redemption 2

Made my first trip to the Rockies feel strangely familiar. I recalled many scenes from this game when I was travelling around Banff. Impressive narration and game art.

RPG · Choice, turn-based

Baldur's Gate 3

You will never seen such a huge world created by another company. The best CRPG game in this era. The very last romantic scene for game people.

RPG · Story · Choice

The Witcher 3

"People... like to invent monsters and monstrosities. Then they seem less monstrous themselves."

Narrative · Choice

Life is Strange

Motivated me to get my first polaroid camera. The first game that I played without chinese subtitles.

Narrative · Survival

This War of Mine

Shows cruel perspective of wars. The art style and game style is very new when it was published. As always 11 bits studio made a deep story that tests human choices.

Narrative · Zombies

The Walking Dead

Still. Not. Bitten.

openworld · action

Black Myth: Wukong

Tell me, who truly attained the scriptures in this world?

Open world · Sci-fi

Cyberpunk 2077

A big dream fro the future, the dlc is actually better than the game story itself, as always the style from CDPR, just like the witcher 3.

Open world · Exploration

Death Stranding

Build true connection between people. Even though we do not see each other from this world, but it saves many of us during the pandamic.

Co-op · Puzzle

It Takes Two

Who said Cooporation mini games can not be a delicate game? a masterpiece that tells people what is game.

Coop · shootinggame

Rainbow Six Siege

I hate this game tbh, but it influenced me too much.

Co-op · Boss hunts

Monster Hunter

Great art, great imangination.

openworld · explore

OuterWilds

I hesitated to put this one under openworld or narrative. The narration is so great that I truely feel likw an astronaut exploring the world, and trying to figure out what is in this universe. The true sprit of exploration.
